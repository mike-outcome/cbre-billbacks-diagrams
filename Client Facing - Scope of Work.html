Overview

The problem we're solving

Tim spends roughly three hours each month manually moving meter readings into a 154-row, 603-column engineering workbook, calculating tenant charges across chilled water, hot water, domestic water, gas, and electricity, then producing a breakdown document for every tenant. The math is correct but the process is fragile, time-consuming, and impossible to scale across the portfolio.

This scope replaces that monthly work with an automated system. Tim emails the meter exports to a dedicated address. The system handles ingestion, calculation, and document generation. Tenant breakdowns land in his inbox — ready for the property management team.

What We Are Building

Capabilities delivered in Phase 1
1
Automated Meter Data Ingestion
Domestic water and BTU meter exports are received via a dedicated email inbox. No manual upload, no spreadsheet opening.
2
Engineering Logic, Codified
The chiller plant, hot water plant, AHU, and exhaust fan formulas from your workbook are translated into reusable system logic.
3
Per-Tenant Charge Calculation
Building loads are allocated to each tenant by airflow percentage. Five utility rates are applied to convert consumption into dollar charges.
4
Tenant Breakdown Documents
12 statements per month, formatted like a standard utility bill, including current-month charges and prior-month comparison.
5
Annual Rate Refresh
When ComEd, Nicor, or City Water rates change, Tim emails the updated workbook. The system refreshes rates — no rebuild required.
6
Configurable Delivery
Statements can be sent individually, in bulk, or routed directly to the property management team for tenant ledger attachment.

The Exchange

What you provide · What you receive

What we need from CBRE

  • BTU Meter Report Monthly Chilled water and hot water consumption by floor / tenant, exported from the building's BAS.
  • Domestic Water Report Monthly 16 domestic water meters, gallon readings by floor / tenant.
  • HVAC Cost Workbook Annual Updated utility rates from ComEd, Nicor, and City Water. Sent at setup and whenever rates change.
  • Consistent Meter Layout Tenant order and column structure remain stable month over month so the system reads the files reliably.

What you receive back

  • 12 Tenant Breakdown Documents Monthly One per tenant suite. Current-month charges, prior-month comparison, and a per-line breakdown showing how each total was calculated.
  • Delivered to Tim's Inbox Statements land automatically at the same time each month. No portal logins or manual exports.
  • PM-Team Ready Format Documents match the format the property management team already attaches to tenant rent statements.
  • Audit-Ready Calculation Trail Every charge ties back to the underlying meter reading, allocation percentage, and applicable utility rate.

Delivery Plan

Phased rollout
Phase 1 Target: April 13, 2026

Replace the manual spreadsheet, end to end

Everything required to retire the current monthly process at 400 North Aberdeen:

  • Email-based ingestion of BTU and DW reports
  • Full calculation chain replicating the HVAC Cost Workbook formulas
  • Five utility rates applied automatically (electricity commodity, electricity demand, natural gas, water, blended electricity)
  • 12 tenant breakdown documents generated and delivered each month
  • Annual rate refresh workflow
Phase 2 Future scope

Intelligence layer on top of the automation

Once Phase 1 is operational and producing a clean monthly history, the following extensions are available:

  • Anomaly detection — alerts when a tenant's charge falls outside an expected range (e.g. total below $2,000 or month-over-month spike >20%)
  • Historical benchmarking dashboards — month-over-month and year-over-year trend reporting
  • Direct BAS integration via the Aero Performance Group API (replacing email-based ingestion)
  • Embedded charts and graphs inside the tenant statement
  • Optional tenant payment integration (Stripe / Apple Pay)

Dependencies & Constraints

What this scope assumes
Meter exports remain consistent
The BAS continues to produce the same BTU and DW reports, with tenant order and column structure unchanged month over month.
Tim sends rate updates when they change
Utility rates from ComEd, Nicor, and City Water are refreshed whenever Tim emails an updated HVAC Cost Workbook (typically annually).
Phase 1 covers 400 North Aberdeen only
The system is designed to extend to other buildings, but Phase 1 scope is limited to the 12 tenants currently at 400 NA.
Work order billbacks are out of scope
Phase 1 covers metered utility consumption only. After-hours HVAC billing, work order billbacks, and accounting system integration are not included.
